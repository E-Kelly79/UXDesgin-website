About Comheragh Mountains

The Comeraghs are an easy to access mountain range, offering wonderful vistas across County Waterford, even as far as the sea. The Comeraghs offer everything from a dramatic waterfall down a cliff face, to ‘magic road’, a natural phenomenon where up is down – this needs to be experienced to be believed.

The marked hiking trails provide their users with a choice of two to four and a half hour walks of a moderate or strenuous level of difficulty and an opportunity to experience Waterford’s Natural Heritage of the Comeragh Mountains in a sustainable manner.

Visitors have plenty to take in – from the dramatic scenery of the Nire valley to the spectacular Coomshingaun corrie lake. Small and friendly villages such as Rathgormack, are dotted in and around the mountain range. The wonderful Mahon Falls is a must but everyone can find their own bit of heaven in the Comeraghs.

Whether you prefer to wander through quiet forests, discover trails where it appears no one has walked for thousands of years or stay on the beaten path, ambling along signposted looped walks, the beautiful Comeraghs are a must-see for any visitor to the Deise.

Activities

  • Mahon Falls

    Mahon Falls is an 80m waterfall nestled in the Comeragh Mountains near Lemybrien, County Waterford. There is a car park close to the falls with a not too strenuous walk to get to them. The dramatic waterfall which is surrounded by breathtaking beauty makes this a trip not to be missed.

  • The Magic Road

    On a road near the Mahon Falls in County Waterford, Ireland there is a magical road with a fairy tree. If you park a car at the bottom of the hill, next to the fairy tree. The car will roll UP the hill . AMAZING !. You must experencice this to belive it.

  • Coumshingaun Lake

    Coumshingaun, the jewel of the Comeraghs, the glacial lake said to be one of the finest examples of a corrie lake (coum in gaelic) in Western Europe. There are nine lakes or coums to explore in the Comeragh mountains, all beautiful but none compare to the majesty of Coumshingaun.

  • Quad Biking

    The Lafcadio Hearn Japanese Gardens in Tramore have a wonderful and unique story to tell. In their style and planting they contain elements of the gardening traditions of the countries and cultures traversed by Hearn durning his varied life
